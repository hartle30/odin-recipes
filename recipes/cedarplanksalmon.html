Cedar Planked Salmon

A delicious Asian-inspired salmon dish grilled on a cedar plank

This dish will make your mouth water and your taste buds tingle! A simple soy-based marinade adds incredible flavor while being quick enough to make on a weeknight while your children scream obscenities at you. The cedar plank offers some smokiness without imparting the taste of a California wildfire. Perfect paired with an over-priced Napa Valley Chardonnay. Yum!

Ingredients

  • 3 untreated cedar planks
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 2 (2 pound) salmon fillets, skin removed

Steps

  1. Soak cedar planks for at least one hour in warm water.
  2. Stir together soy sauce, vegetable oil, rice viegar, sesame oil, green onions, ginger, and garlic in a shallow bowl.
  3. Place salmon fillets in soy mixture and turn to coat. Cover and marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to one hour in refrigerator.
  4. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium heat. Place planks on grill grate and heat until they start to smoke and crackle just a little.
  5. Remove salmon from marinade and place on planks. Discard marinade.
  6. Close grill cover. Grill until salmon flakes easily with a fork; about 20 minutes. Salmon will continue to cook after you remove it from the grill.
